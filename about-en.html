About Ursula


Ursula studied contemporary stage dance and dance pedagogy at the Anton Bruckner University Linz (AT) and contemporary dance with a focus on creation at the University of Performing Arts La Manufacture in Lausanne (CH).

As part of her training, she worked with artists such as Thomas Hauert, Fabrice Mazliah, Jeremy Nelson, Martin Kilvady, David Zambrano, Mal Pelo, Àngels Margarit, Michael Turinsky and Kirstie Simson, among others. Ursula performed in Switzerland at the STEPS Festival, in Paris, Graz, Linz, Vienna and Girona.

2019, she received the scholarship Startstipendium for performing arts from the Austrian federal ministry for arts and culture. Today, she lives and works in Graz (AT) and is also active in Catalonia (ES).

In her dance performances she deals with topics such as feminism, language and identity. She always looks at her creations from a sociological point of view and they are characterized by humor, sensuality and a strong physicality.

Her work has been invited to the festivals spleen*trieb, newsOFFstyria and the Center Cívic Ter (Girona 2021). In 2022 she has been invited to the Viertelfestival NÖ and the Homotopia Festival in Liverpool. Also in 2022 she receives the Morgenstern prize of the State of Styria (AT). She works collectively with different artists to realize her projects.
